CODE JUNKIE

Code Competition

CODE JUNKIE

THE OBJECTIVE OF THIS EVENT IS TO TEST THE PROFICIENCY OF THE PARTICIPANT IN C PROGRAMMING LANGUAGE. THE EVENT COVERS THE BASIC PRINCIPLES OF C PROGRAMMING LANGUAGE.

  • PRIZE MONEY : RS.20000/-

STRUCTURE

ABOUT EVENT

ROUND 1:

Multiple choice questions (MCQ) round. Time allotted is 30 minutes. Negative marking will be implemented. Further details will be informed at the time of the event.

ROUND 2:

The top teams from the Round 1 will qualify for this round. This round will be a rapid coding round where teams have to complete codes within the stipulated time. The output file needs to be submitted and will be judged on the basis of the time taken to code it. Further details will be informed at the time of event.

ROUND 3:

The top teams from Round 2 will qualify for this round. This round will also be a rapid coding round and relatively difficult questions will be asked. The code must be working as well as efficient. All the possible inputs and corner cases should be handled. The code will be judged on the basis of efficiency of the code and the time required to code it. Further details will be informed at the time of the event.

TEAM AND FEE STRUCTURE:

Team Size : Max 2 participants per team. Registration Fee : ₹ 50 per team.

RULES

RULES

1. The environment will either be Ubuntu 14.04 OS with gedit/vim/emacs as a text editor and gcc as a compiler OR Windows 7 with Turbo C / Dev C++ (as per the team’s choice).

2. Participants should be First Year or Second Year Undergraduate students only.

3. Participants need to bring their College ID cards and registration receipts during reporting.

4. Participants are expected to give their correct contact details, so as to inform them about the results.

5. Rules may be changed without prior intimation. Participants are requested to check the MindSpark’19 website www.mind-spark.org regularly for updates

6. In case of any disputes, the decision of the organizers and the judges will be final and binding to all.

7. Team members may be from different colleges / institutions but one participant may not be a part of multiple teams for the same event.

FAQS

Q. What is the team structure?

Ans. A team may consist of maximum 2 participants. The members of the team can be from different educational institutions but should be strictly First Year or Second Year Undergraduates.

Q. Do I have to bring anything?

Ans.College ID Cards and registration receipts are compulsory; you may carry pen or pencil, if you wish to work out a rough plan for rounds 2 and 3.

Q. Can I participate in other events at MindSpark’19?

Ans. You are allowed to participate in as many events at MindSpark’19 as long as you are able to manage your own schedule.

Q. Which IDE (Integrated Development Environment) will be provided?

Ans. The environment provided will be Ubuntu 14.04 OS with emacs/vim/gedit as text editor and gcc as compiler or Windows 7 OS with Turbo C/ Dev C++, depending on choice of participating teams. COEP recommends Linux.
